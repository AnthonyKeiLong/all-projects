Developmental Research Methodology

Unlike experimental psychology, developmental psychology grapples heavily with the variable of time. Understanding how to isolate age effects from generational differences is a core Year 2 competency.

Design Description Major Vulnerability Time/Cost
Cross-Sectional Compares individuals of different ages at a single point in time. Cohort Effects (Generational differences) Low / Fast
Longitudinal Follows the same group of individuals over a long period. Attrition (Drop-out rates), Practice Effects High / Slow
Sequential Combines cross-sectional and longitudinal approaches. Highly complex data analysis, expensive. Very High
