The Concept of Equilibrium

A dynamic equilibrium occurs in a closed system when the rate of the forward reaction equals the rate of the backward reaction. The macroscopic properties (like concentration and color) remain constant.

The Equilibrium Constant (K_c)

aA + bB ⇌ cC + dD

Kc = [C]c[D]d [A]a[B]b

Kc > 1 (Products Favored)

The equilibrium position lies to the right. The yield of products is high.

Kc ≈ 1 (Balanced)

Significant amounts of both reactants and products exist in the equilibrium mixture.

Kc < 1 (Reactants Favored)

The equilibrium position lies to the left. The yield of products is low.

The Standard Setup (Perfect Square)

Let's look at the classic reaction between Hydrogen and Iodine forming Hydrogen Iodide. If initial concentrations are equal, the expression simplifies beautifully.

H₂(g) + I₂(g) ⇌ 2HI(g)

Step [H₂] [I₂] [HI]
Initial (mol dm⁻³) 0.500 0.500 0.000
Change (mol dm⁻³) - x - x + 2x
Equilibrium 0.500 - x 0.500 - x 2x
DSE Question: Perfect Square

"A mixture of 0.500 mol of H₂(g) and 0.500 mol of I₂(g) is allowed to reach equilibrium in a closed container of volume 1.00 dm³ at 430 °C. The equilibrium constant, Kc, for the reaction H₂(g) + I₂(g) ⇌ 2HI(g) at this temperature is 54.3. Calculate the equilibrium concentration of HI(g). (3 marks)"

// Step-by-Step Solution:
1. Kc = [HI]² / ([H₂][I₂]) = 54.3
2. Substitute: (2x)² / (0.500 - x)² = 54.3
3. Take square root on both sides:
√54.3 = 2x / (0.500 - x)
7.369 = 2x / (0.500 - x)
4. Solve for x:
3.6845 - 7.369x = 2x
9.369x = 3.6845 → x = 0.393
5. [HI] = 2x = 2(0.393) = 0.786 mol dm⁻³

Scenario 2 Missing Reactants (Reverse Shift)

If a reactant is missing, the reaction must shift left. Products decrease (-x) and reactants increase (+x).

DSE Question: Shift Left

"0.500 mol of PCl₃(g) and 0.500 mol of Cl₂(g) are injected into an evacuated 10.0 dm³ closed vessel. For the reaction PCl₅(g) ⇌ PCl₃(g) + Cl₂(g), the equilibrium constant Kc is 0.0211 mol dm⁻³. Calculate the equilibrium concentration of PCl₅(g). (3 marks)"

// 1. Find Initial Concentration
[PCl₃] = [Cl₂] = 0.500mol / 10.0dm³ = 0.050 mol dm⁻³
// 2. Setup (Shifting Left)
Eq [PCl₅] = 0 + x = x
Eq [PCl₃] = 0.050 - x
Eq [Cl₂] = 0.050 - x
// 3. Solve
0.0211 = (0.050 - x)² / x
0.0211x = 0.0025 - 0.10x + x²
x² - 0.1211x + 0.0025 = 0
x ≈ 0.0255 mol dm⁻³ = [PCl₅]
Scenario 3 "Small x" Approximation (Weak Acid)

Often seen in Topic 11 (Acid-Base). When Ka is very small, the degree of ionization is tiny: (Initial - x) ≈ Initial.

DSE Question: Weak Acid Ka

"An aqueous solution of ethanoic acid (CH₃COOH) has a concentration of 0.100 mol dm⁻³. At 298 K, Ka = 1.8 × 10⁻⁵ mol dm⁻³. Calculate the equilibrium concentration of H⁺(aq). (Assume degree of ionization is small). (3 marks)"

// Setup: CH₃COOH ⇌ CH₃COO⁻ + H⁺
Eq [CH₃COOH] = 0.100 - x ≈ 0.100
Eq [CH₃COO⁻] = x
Eq [H⁺] = x
// Solve with Approximation
Ka = [CH₃COO⁻][H⁺] / [CH₃COOH]
1.8 × 10⁻⁵ = (x)(x) / 0.100
x² = 1.8 × 10⁻⁶
x = 1.34 × 10⁻³ mol dm⁻³ = [H⁺]
