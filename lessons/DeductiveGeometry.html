Introduction to Deductive Geometry

Deductive geometry is a formal system of mathematics where we prove geometric facts (called Theorems) using a step-by-step logical process. We start from a few basic assumptions (called Axioms) and previously proven theorems.

Every statement in a geometric proof must be supported by a valid reason. This builds an unbreakable chain of logic.

Triangle Properties

1. Angle Sum of Triangle

The sum of the interior angles of any triangle is always $180^\circ$.

$$\angle A + \angle B + \angle C = 180^\circ$$

Reason: $\angle$ sum of $\triangle$

2. Exterior Angle of Triangle

The exterior angle of a triangle equals the sum of the two opposite interior angles.

$$Ext. \angle = Int. \angle_1 + Int. \angle_2$$

Reason: ext. $\angle$ of $\triangle$
